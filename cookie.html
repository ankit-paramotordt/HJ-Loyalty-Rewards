Cookie Policy

Effective Date: September 17, 2025

1. Introduction

1.1 This Cookie Policy explains how HJ Reward Limited (“we,” “us,” or “our”) uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit our website, mobile applications, and related platforms (the “Site”) in connection with the HJ Reward Program (the “Program”).
1.2 By using the Site or participating in the Program, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

2. What Are Cookies?

2.1 Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone, etc.) when you visit the Site.
2.2 Cookies allow us to:
2.2.1 Recognize your device and login status;
2.2.2 Track interactions and usage;
2.2.3 Secure your session;
2.2.4 Personalize your experience.
2.3 Types of cookies may include:
2.3.1 Session cookies – temporary and deleted once you close your browser;
2.3.2 Persistent cookies – stored on your device for a set period.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 We use the following categories of cookies:
3.1.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies – required for the Site to function (e.g., login, account access, fraud prevention);
3.1.2 Performance & Analytics Cookies – collect usage data to help us improve Site speed, stability, and features;
3.1.3 Functionality Cookies – store your preferences (e.g., language, currency, login settings);
3.1.4 Targeting/Advertising Cookies – track browsing habits and deliver personalized offers or ads;
3.1.5 Third-Party Cookies – set by service providers such as analytics, payment processors, and ad networks.

4. How We Use Cookies

4.1 We use cookies for the following purposes:
4.1.1 To enable secure login and prevent fraudulent activity;
4.1.2 To remember your preferences and personalize your account;
4.1.3 To measure traffic, user behaviour, and improve Site performance;
4.1.4 To provide targeted rewards and advertisements relevant to you;
4.1.5 To comply with legal and regulatory requirements.
4.2 The lawful basis for processing cookie-related personal data is as follows:
4.2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies – processed on the basis of our legitimate interests in ensuring the security and functionality of the Site;
4.2.2 Analytics, Functionality, and Advertising Cookies – processed on the basis of your explicit consent obtained through our cookie banner.

5. Cookie Duration

5.1 Cookies remain active for different periods:
5.1.1 Session cookies – expire when you close the browser;
5.1.2 Persistent cookies – remain for [e.g., 30 days / 6 months / 2 years depending on purpose].
5.2 Retention periods are determined based on the necessity of the cookie for the purpose for which it is used. Once the retention period expires, cookies are automatically deleted or anonymised in line with our data protection obligations. Retention is limited to what is strictly necessary for the stated purposes.

7. Consent and Control

7.1 We will not place non-essential cookies (including analytics, functionality, or advertising cookies) on your device until you provide your explicit consent through our cookie banner or settings.
7.2 You may:
7.2.1 Accept or reject non-essential cookies (analytics, ads);
7.2.2 Withdraw or change your cookie preferences at any time via the Cookie Settings link in the footer.
7.2.3 Withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

8. Managing and Deleting Cookies

8.1 You can manage cookies in the following ways:
8.1.1 Through your browser settings (clear or block cookies);
8.1.2 Via industry opt-out tools:
8.1.2.1 Network Advertising Initiative;
8.1.2.2 Digital Advertising Alliance;
8.1.2.3 Your Online Choices (EU).
8.2 Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of the Site.

9. Third-Party Cookies & Data Transfers

9.1 Some cookies are set by trusted third-party providers such as analytics, hosting, and payment gateways.
9.2 These may involve transferring cookie-related data outside Mauritius.
9.3 Such transfers will only take place where an adequate level of protection is ensured in compliance with the Data Protection Act 2017, for example through adequacy decisions, contractual safeguards, or Standard Contractual Clauses where applicable.

10. Privacy and Data Protection

10.1 Under the Data Protection Act 2017, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data collected through cookies:
10.1.1 Right to access and obtain a copy of your personal data;
10.1.2 Right to request rectification of inaccurate or incomplete data;
10.1.3 Right to request erasure of your data (“right to be forgotten”);
10.1.4 Right to restrict or object to processing, including for direct marketing;
10.1.5 Right to data portability where applicable;
10.1.6 Right to withdraw consent to the use of cookies at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
10.1.7 Right to Lodge a Complaint, to file a complaint with the Data Protection Commissioner of Mauritius or the relevant data protection authority in your jurisdiction.
10.2 To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@hjreward.com.
10.3 For more details on how we collect, use, and protect your personal data, please refer to our Privacy Policy available on the HJ Rewards Website.”

11. Children’s Data

11.1 Our Site and Program are not directed at children under 18.
11.2 We do not knowingly place cookies to profile children.
11.3 If you believe we have collected such information, please contact us to request deletion.

12. Changes to This Cookie Policy

12.1 We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time.
12.2 The revised version will be posted here with an updated Effective Date.
12.3 We encourage you to check this page regularly.

13. Applicable Law

13.1 This Cookie Policy is governed by and shall be interpreted in accordance with the Data Protection Act 2017 of Mauritius (“DPA 2017”) and any other applicable local regulations.
13.2 Where we process data of individuals located in other jurisdictions, we also comply with relevant international data protection standards, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
